About Malcolm Turnbull

Malcolm Bligh Turnbull (born 24 October 1954) is an Australian former politician who was the 29th Prime Minister of Australia from 2015 to 2018. He served twice as Leader of the Liberal Party, firstly from 2008 to 2009 when he was also Leader of the Opposition, and a second time from 2015 to 2018. He was the MP for Wentworth in the House of Representatives from 2004 to 2018.

Malcom Bligh Turnbull was born in Sydney, new south Wales on 24 october 1954. His father was a hotel broker and his mother was a radio actor. Turnbull spent his first years of school at Vaucluse Public School and then he attended Grammar’s high school campus on college Street, during his time he lived at the boarding facilities. He graduated from the university at sydney with a Bachelor of Arts and a Bachelor of laws. He attended Oxford earning a Bachelor of Civil Law. Before entering politics. Turnbull worked as a journalist and lawyer. On 1989 he married Lucy hughes who was the Lord Major of the sydney . They later had two children: Alex and Daisy. Nowadays they three grandchildrens. During his politic career he served as Chair of the Australian Republican Movement from 1993 to 2000. He did not have success during the “yes” camping in the 1999 republic referendum. turnbull was the minister for environment and water from january 2007 until December 2007. After becoming 2nd in the 2007 leadership election,Turnbull became the leader of the opposition. Turnbull was defeated on a leadership challenge by Tony Abbott in 2009. He planned to leave politics after this but Turnbull decided to stay and was later appointed minister from communications in the 2013 federal election Turnbull resigned from the cabinet on September 2015 and challenged Abbott reclaiming the leadership of the Liberal party. He was sworn in as Prime Minister of Australia the following days On 27 August 2018 Turnbull announced that he would be resigning from Parliament, triggering a 2018 wentworth by election.
